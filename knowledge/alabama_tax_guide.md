# Alabama Tax Guide — What You Need to Know

## The Big Win: Alabama Doesn't Tax W-2 Wages the Way You Think

Alabama has state income tax, but it's **significantly reduced** for wage earners because:
- Alabama allows you to **deduct federal income tax paid** from your state taxable income
- This is rare — most states don't allow this
- Result: your effective Alabama tax rate on wages is much lower than the posted rates

## Alabama Overtime Pay Exemption (Act 2023-421)

### What It Is
Alabama exempts overtime pay from state income tax for qualifying employees.

### Key Details
| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Effective dates | January 1, 2024 — June 30, 2025 |
| Who qualifies | Full-time hourly wage employees working over 40 hours/week |
| Who doesn't qualify | Salaried employees, employees whose wages are subject to another state's withholding |
| What's exempt | The overtime portion of pay (hours worked over 40/week) |
| Employer reporting | Employers must report total OT paid and number of employees who received OT |

### Important Notes
- **This is temporary** — sunsets June 30, 2025 unless the legislature extends it
- For tax year 2025, only OT earned **January 1 – June 30** qualifies
- OT earned July 1 – December 31, 2025 is taxable in Alabama (unless extended)
- Your W-2 Box 14 may show "AL OT" — this is the exempt amount
- **Check if your tax software applies this automatically** — many don't, and you may need to enter it as a manual Alabama income adjustment

### How to Claim It
1. Calculate your OT pay earned during Jan 1 – June 30, 2025
2. In your Alabama return, look for income adjustments/subtractions
3. Enter the exempt OT amount as a subtraction from Alabama income
4. Keep pay stubs documenting OT hours and dates

### Tax Savings Example
| Annual OT (Jan-June only) | AL Tax Saved (~5%) |
|---------------------------|-------------------|
| $5,000 | $250 |
| $10,000 | $500 |
| $15,000 | $750 |
| $20,000 | $1,000 |

## Alabama and Military/Veterans
- **Military retirement pay**: Fully exempt from Alabama income tax
- **VA disability benefits**: Not taxable (federal or state)
- **Active duty pay**: Exempt from Alabama tax if stationed outside Alabama

## Other Alabama Tax Facts
- **No sales tax on groceries?** Wrong — Alabama is one of the few states that DOES tax groceries (state rate: 4%, plus local)
- **Property tax**: Alabama has one of the lowest property tax rates in the country
- **Vehicle ad valorem tax**: The ad valorem portion of your annual vehicle registration may be deductible on your federal return (if you itemize)
- **Federal tax deduction**: Alabama is one of only 6 states that lets you deduct federal income tax from state taxable income

## Filing Tips for Alabama
1. Always check whether your tax software handles the federal tax deduction correctly
2. If you worked overtime in 2024-2025, verify the OT exemption is applied
3. Alabama filing deadline typically matches federal (April 15)
4. Alabama e-file is available through most major tax software
5. If you owe, payment options are available through My Alabama Taxes (MAT) portal
