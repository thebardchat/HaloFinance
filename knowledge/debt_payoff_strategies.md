# Debt Payoff Strategies — Plain English Guide

## The Three Methods

### 1. Avalanche Method (Mathematically Optimal)
**Pay off highest interest rate first.**

- Line up all debts by APR, highest to lowest
- Pay minimums on everything
- Throw all extra money at the highest-APR debt
- When it's gone, move to the next highest

**Pros:** Saves the most money in interest over time
**Cons:** If the highest-rate debt is also the biggest balance, it can take months to feel any progress

### 2. Snowball Method (Psychologically Powerful)
**Pay off smallest balance first.**

- Line up all debts by balance, smallest to largest
- Pay minimums on everything
- Throw all extra money at the smallest balance
- When it's gone, roll that payment into the next smallest

**Pros:** Quick wins build momentum. Freed-up payments snowball fast.
**Cons:** You might pay more total interest if small debts have low rates

### 3. Hybrid Method (Best of Both — Recommended)
**Pay off smallest HIGH-INTEREST debts first.**

- Group debts into tiers by interest rate (high/medium/low)
- Within the high-interest tier, attack smallest balances first
- Get the quick wins AND minimize interest

**Why this works:** When all your rates are similar (25-35% range), the interest difference between avalanche and snowball is tiny — but the psychological boost of killing debts quickly is massive.

## The Snowball Effect (Why It Accelerates)

Every debt you pay off frees up its minimum payment. That freed money rolls into the next debt.

**Example progression:**
| Debts Remaining | Extra Money Available | Snowball Speed |
|-----------------|----------------------|----------------|
| 10 debts | $300/mo extra | Slow start |
| 8 debts | $300 + $75 freed = $375/mo | Building |
| 5 debts | $375 + $150 freed = $525/mo | Rolling |
| 3 debts | $525 + $200 freed = $725/mo | Crushing it |
| 1 debt | $725 + $300 freed = $1,025/mo | Avalanche |

The snowball gets bigger and faster as you go. The hardest part is the beginning.

## Before You Start Paying: Stop the Bleeding

These actions cost $0 and free up money immediately:

### 1. Cancel Credit Protection Plans
Those $7-20/month "credit protection" or "payment protection" add-ons on credit cards are nearly worthless. They add up fast:
- 5 cards × $12/mo average = $60/month = $720/year
- That's $720 you could throw at debt

### 2. Cancel Unnecessary Fees
- Paper statement fees ($2-5/mo per card) — switch to paperless
- Account maintenance fees — call and ask for waiver or close the card after payoff
- Annual fees on cards with no benefits — consider if the card is worth keeping

### 3. Stop Using the Cards
- Freeze them (literally — in a bag of water in the freezer)
- Remove them from online shopping accounts
- Delete them from Apple Pay / Google Pay
- If you can't pay cash, you can't afford it during payoff mode

## The Math: How to Calculate Your Payoff Timeline

### Step 1: Add up all minimum payments
All your monthly minimums across all debts = your baseline obligation.

### Step 2: Find extra money
- W-4 adjustment (if you're getting a big tax refund, you're overpaying)
- Cancel fees and subscriptions
- Side income / overtime
- Tax refund (one-time boost)
- Sell stuff you don't need

### Step 3: Total monthly attack budget
Minimums + extra money = your total debt attack budget.

### Step 4: Project the timeline
- Divide smallest debt by your extra money = months to kill it
- Add that debt's minimum to your extra money
- Repeat for each debt
- The snowball accelerates — later debts die much faster

## Debt Payoff Phases

### Phase 1: Quick Kills (debts under $200)
- These die fast with any extra money
- Each one killed frees up a payment
- Momentum builder

### Phase 2: Mid-Range (debts $200-$600)
- Snowball is building speed
- Each kill feels bigger
- You're visibly making progress

### Phase 3: Big Balances ($600+)
- Snowball is now a boulder
- $500-1,000+/month hitting one debt at a time
- These fall faster than you'd expect

## Windfalls: How to Use Them

When you get a tax refund, bonus, or unexpected money:

| Priority | Action | Why |
|----------|--------|-----|
| 0 | Get any past-due secured debts current | Prevent repo/foreclosure |
| 1 | Honor your values (tithe, family) | Non-negotiable |
| 2 | Minimum emergency fund ($1,000-1,500) | Prevents new debt from emergencies |
| 3 | Kill as many small debts as possible | Maximizes freed monthly payments |
| 4 | Attack next target in payoff order | Keep the snowball rolling |

**Do it the day the money hits.** Hesitation = spent.

## Credit Cards: Minimum Payment Trap

If you pay only minimums on a $1,000 credit card at 28% APR:
- Monthly minimum: ~$25
- Time to pay off: **17+ years**
- Total paid: **~$3,200** (you paid $2,200 in interest on $1,000)

Even $50/month extra cuts that to ~18 months and saves ~$1,800 in interest.

## Buy Now Pay Later (BNPL) — The Hidden Debt

Affirm, Klarna, Afterpay loans look harmless but:
- APRs of 30-36% are common — same as credit cards
- Multiple small loans add up to a big monthly burden
- They don't show on credit reports (so you can't track utilization)
- "4 easy payments" on 10 items = 40 payments that drain your account

Treat BNPL the same as credit card debt. Pay it off in priority order by APR and balance.

## Rules During Payoff Mode

1. **No new debt.** Period. If you can't pay cash, wait.
2. **Pay minimums on everything.** Never miss a minimum — late fees + credit damage make things worse.
3. **All extra money hits one target.** Spreading extra across all debts slows you down.
4. **Every payoff gets celebrated** — then immediately rolled into the next debt.
5. **Review monthly.** Update balances, adjust the plan, track your wins.
6. **Protect the essentials.** Mortgage, car payment, food, utilities come first. Always.

## When All Consumer Debt Is Gone

Once you've killed all credit card + BNPL debt:
1. **Build a real emergency fund** (3-6 months of expenses)
2. **Increase retirement contributions** (you've been "paying" this much to debt — now pay yourself)
3. **Attack the car loan** with the same snowball
4. **Start investing** beyond retirement accounts
5. **Never go back.** The monthly freedom you now have is worth more than anything you could buy on credit.

## The One Thing That Matters

Progress, not perfection. If you can only throw $50 extra at debt this month, that's $50 more than the minimum. The snowball starts small. Trust the math. It works.
