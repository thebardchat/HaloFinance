# Claiming a Parent (or Other Relative) as a Dependent — Plain English Guide

## Why This Matters

If you financially support a parent, grandparent, or other relative, you may be able to claim them as a **qualifying relative** on your tax return. This gets you:
- **Other Dependent Credit**: $500 per qualifying relative
- **Alabama dependent exemption**: $1,000 (reduces state taxable income)
- **Potential medical expense deduction**: If you pay their medical bills and itemize

## The Four Tests (All Must Pass)

### 1. Not a Qualifying Child
Your parent can't be claimed as your "qualifying child" (they're your parent, not your child — this test is automatic for parents).

### 2. Relationship Test ✓
Must be related to you OR live with you all year. Parents, grandparents, aunts, uncles, in-laws all qualify by relationship — they don't even need to live with you.

### 3. Gross Income Test
Their **gross income** must be under **$5,050** (2025).

**What counts as gross income:**
- Wages, interest, dividends, rental income, pensions (taxable portion)

**What does NOT count:**
- Social Security benefits (excluded from gross income for this test)
- VA disability benefits (tax-free, not gross income)
- Tax-exempt interest

**This means:** A parent who receives only Social Security + VA disability can have $0 gross income for this test, even if they receive $20,000+/year in combined benefits.

### 4. Support Test
**You must provide more than 50% of their total support for the year.**

This is where most people get tripped up. Here's how to calculate it:

#### What Counts as Support You Provide:
| Category | How to Calculate |
|----------|-----------------|
| Housing | Fair rental value of room/space (not your mortgage payment) |
| Food | Reasonable estimate of their share of groceries + meals |
| Medical/prescriptions | What you actually pay out of pocket |
| Utilities | Their proportional share |
| Transportation | Gas, rides, their car expenses you pay |
| Clothing | What you buy for them |
| Personal items | Toiletries, phone, entertainment |

#### What Counts as Their Own Support:
| Source | Counts? |
|--------|---------|
| Social Security spent on their own needs | Yes — counts as THEIR support |
| VA disability spent on their own needs | Yes — counts as THEIR support |
| Their savings spent on themselves | Yes |
| Benefits they receive (Medicare, Medicaid) | Yes — counts as THEIR support |
| Money they give YOU for household expenses | Tricky — see below |

#### The Tricky Part: When They Contribute to the Household
If your parent gives you their Social Security check to help with rent/bills, does that count as YOUR support or THEIRS?

**IRS position:** Money they spend on their own support (food they eat, roof over their head) counts as THEIR support — even if the check goes to you first. But money that goes to shared household expenses benefits everyone.

**Best practice:** Track what portion of their income goes to their personal support vs. general household. This is a gray area — consult a CPA if it's close.

### Support Test Calculation Example

| Item | Your Cost | Their Resources |
|------|-----------|-----------------|
| Housing (fair rental) | $750/mo = $9,000 | $0 |
| Food (their share) | $425/mo = $5,100 | $0 |
| Medical | $150/mo = $1,800 | $0 |
| Utilities (their share) | $150/mo = $1,800 | $0 |
| Transportation | $100/mo = $1,200 | $0 |
| Clothing/personal | $40/mo = $480 | $0 |
| Their SS (contributed back) | — | $14,400 |
| **TOTALS** | **$19,380** | **$14,400** |

- Total support: $19,380 + $14,400 = $33,780
- Your share: $19,380 / $33,780 = **57.4%**
- Required: More than 50%
- **Result: PASSES** ✓

## Tax Benefits Summary

| Benefit | Amount | Type |
|---------|--------|------|
| Other Dependent Credit (federal) | $500 | Nonrefundable credit |
| Dependent exemption (Alabama) | $1,000 | Reduces state taxable income |
| Medical expenses | Variable | Only if itemizing AND exceeding 7.5% of AGI |

## Documentation to Keep

- [ ] Their SSA-1099 (Social Security statement)
- [ ] VA disability rating letter and payment records
- [ ] Bank statements showing your support payments
- [ ] Medical bills and receipts you paid
- [ ] Housing cost documentation (fair rental value comparable)
- [ ] Any other income statements they receive

## When to Get a CPA Involved

- Your support percentage is close to 50% (between 48-55%)
- The dependent has significant VA disability or other non-taxable income
- Multiple family members share support (Multiple Support Agreement may be needed)
- The dependent has gross income close to the $5,050 limit
- You're not sure how to value housing or other in-kind support

## Common Mistakes

1. **Forgetting that SS counts as their support** — even though SS is excluded from the gross income test, money they spend from SS counts as their own support
2. **Not tracking support throughout the year** — start a simple log now, don't try to reconstruct it in April
3. **Claiming a parent who supports themselves** — if they pay their own rent and food, you probably don't provide >50%
4. **Double-counting** — if two family members both claim to support a parent, only one can claim the credit
5. **Confusing the gross income test with total income** — SS and VA disability don't count toward the $5,050 limit
