# IRS Identity Protection PIN (IP PIN) Guide

## What Is an IP PIN?

A 6-digit number the IRS assigns to you that must be included on your tax return to verify your identity. If your return is rejected for a missing IP PIN, you can't file until you get one.

## Why You Might Need One

- The IRS assigned you one after an identity theft case
- You requested one for extra security
- Someone else filed a return using your SSN
- The IRS flagged your account for identity verification

## How to Get Your IP PIN

### Option 1: Online (Fastest — 15 minutes)
1. Go to **irs.gov/ippin**
2. Click "Get an IP PIN"
3. Create or sign into your **IRS Online Account** (ID.me verification required)
4. Your IP PIN will be displayed on screen
5. **Write it down** — you'll need it for tax filing

### Option 2: By Phone
1. Call **1-800-908-4490**
2. Have your identity information ready (SSN, DOB, address, filing status)
3. They'll verify your identity and provide the PIN
4. Wait times vary — call early morning for shorter waits

### Option 3: In Person
1. Visit a local IRS Taxpayer Assistance Center
2. Bring photo ID and another form of identification
3. They'll verify your identity and issue the PIN
4. Find your nearest office at irs.gov/help/contact-your-local-irs-office

## Critical Details

### Married Filing Jointly (MFJ)
**BOTH spouses may need separate IP PINs.** If either spouse has been assigned an IP PIN, their pin must be on the return. Check with both spouses before filing.

### IP PINs Change Every Year
- A new PIN is issued each January
- Last year's PIN won't work this year
- Get a new one each tax season

### If You Can't Get It Online
The ID.me identity verification can be frustrating. If it fails:
- Try a different browser
- Make sure your phone number and address match IRS records
- Try the phone option (1-800-908-4490)
- Visit an IRS office in person as last resort

## Filing With an IP PIN

### E-Filing
- Your tax software will have a field for IP PIN during the filing process
- Enter the 6-digit number when prompted
- If MFJ, enter both spouses' PINs

### Paper Filing
- Enter the IP PIN in the designated space on the return
- Mail to the address specified in the form instructions

## Common Problems

### "Return Rejected — Missing IP PIN"
- You (or your spouse) have been assigned an IP PIN and didn't include it
- **Fix:** Get your PIN using the methods above, enter it, and refile
- E-filed returns can usually be corrected and resubmitted same day

### "I Never Requested an IP PIN"
- The IRS may have assigned one automatically if they detected suspicious activity on your SSN
- You still need to retrieve it before filing
- This doesn't necessarily mean you were a victim of identity theft

### "I Lost My IP PIN"
- Use the online tool to retrieve it (irs.gov/ippin)
- Call 1-800-908-4490
- You cannot file without it

## Timeline After Getting Your PIN

1. Get IP PIN(s) — same day
2. Enter in tax software and refile — same day
3. IRS accepts return — usually within 24-48 hours
4. Federal refund — 2-3 weeks after acceptance (direct deposit)
5. State refund — 4-6 weeks after acceptance (varies by state)

## Pro Tips

- **Screenshot your IP PIN** and save it somewhere secure
- **Get both spouses' PINs at the same time** if filing MFJ
- **Don't wait until April** — the IRS phone lines get brutal in March-April
- **Opt in voluntarily** if you want extra security — any taxpayer can request one at irs.gov/ippin even if not required
