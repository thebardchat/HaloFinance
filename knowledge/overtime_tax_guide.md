# Overtime Tax Guide — Federal & State

## The Biggest Myth About Overtime

**"Overtime is taxed at a higher rate."**

This is FALSE. Overtime is taxed the same as regular income. It might push some dollars into the next tax bracket, but:
- Only the dollars IN that bracket get the higher rate
- You ALWAYS take home more by working more
- See `tax_brackets_2025.md` for how brackets actually work

## Federal: Working Families Tax Cuts Act (WFTCA)

### What It Is
A federal above-the-line deduction for the **premium portion** of overtime pay. This means the extra 0.5x you earn above your regular rate for OT hours.

### How It Works
| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| What's deductible | The overtime PREMIUM — the extra 0.5x above regular rate |
| Cap (MFJ) | $25,000 |
| Cap (Single) | $12,500 |
| Type | Above-the-line deduction (reduces AGI — you don't need to itemize) |

### Calculating the Premium
Your OT rate is 1.5x your regular rate. The "premium" is the 0.5x bonus portion.

**Example:**
- Regular rate: $25/hr
- OT rate: $37.50/hr (1.5x)
- Premium per OT hour: $12.50 (the 0.5x above regular)
- 20 OT hours/week × 52 weeks = 1,040 OT hours
- Total OT pay: $39,000
- Premium portion: $13,000 (deductible)

### Tax Savings
| OT Premium | Tax Bracket | Tax Saved |
|------------|-------------|-----------|
| $5,000 | 12% | $600 |
| $10,000 | 12% | $1,200 |
| $17,327 | 12% | $2,079 |
| $25,000 (cap) | 12% | $3,000 |
| $25,000 (cap) | 22% | $5,500 |

### Important Notes
- Check whether your tax software applies this automatically
- This deduction reduces AGI, which can also affect eligibility for other credits
- Keep pay stubs documenting OT hours and rates
- This is separate from any state OT exemptions

## State Overtime Tax Breaks

Several states have enacted overtime tax exemptions. These vary significantly:

### Alabama (Act 2023-421)
- **Period:** Jan 1, 2024 – June 30, 2025
- **What:** Full OT pay exempt from state income tax (not just the premium)
- **Who:** Full-time hourly employees working over 40 hrs/week
- **Status:** Temporary — sunsets June 30, 2025
- See `alabama_tax_guide.md` for full details

### Other States to Check
- Several states proposed or passed similar OT exemptions in 2024-2025
- Always check your state's current tax laws — these change frequently
- Your tax software may not automatically apply state OT exemptions

## Stacking Federal + State OT Benefits

If your state has an OT exemption, you can potentially benefit from BOTH:
1. **Federal WFTCA deduction** — deducts the OT premium from federal taxable income
2. **State OT exemption** — exempts OT from state income tax

These are independent — getting one doesn't prevent the other.

### Example: Alabama Worker, $35K Total OT
| Benefit | Amount Exempt/Deducted | Tax Rate | Savings |
|---------|----------------------|----------|---------|
| Federal WFTCA (premium: $17.5K) | $17,500 | 12% | $2,100 |
| AL OT Exemption (Jan-Jun: $17.5K) | $17,500 | ~5% | $875 |
| **Combined savings** | | | **$2,975** |

## Tax Software Warning

Not all tax software handles OT deductions/exemptions correctly:
- **Federal WFTCA**: Newer provision — verify it's in your software's tax year update
- **State OT exemptions**: Often missed by software, especially temporary provisions
- **Always check**: Review your return's income and deduction summaries before filing
- If a deduction seems missing, look for manual adjustment options

## The Bottom Line

Work the overtime. The tax system rewards it more than ever:
1. Federal OT premium deduction saves 12-22% on the premium portion
2. Some states exempt OT from state tax entirely
3. You ALWAYS take home more money by working more hours
4. The "overtime tax" myth has cost American workers billions in lost income
