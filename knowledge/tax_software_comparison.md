# Tax Software Comparison Guide

## The Decision Framework

Don't pick tax software by sticker price. Pick it by **net refund after fees.**

A "free" option that misses a $2,000 deduction costs you $2,000. A $200 option that catches it makes you $1,800. **Cheaper isn't cheaper if it costs you money.**

## How to Compare (Step by Step)

1. **Run your return in two programs** — most let you go all the way to the summary before paying
2. **Compare the bottom line** — total refund or amount owed, not the software price
3. **Check these specific items** (these are where software differs):
   - Child Tax Credit / ACTC
   - Overtime premium deduction (WFTCA)
   - State-specific exemptions (e.g., AL overtime exemption)
   - Investment income (1099-B, crypto)
   - Self-employment deductions
   - Dependent credits (ODC, EITC)
4. **Calculate net: refund minus software cost = what you actually pocket**
5. **Pick the winner**

## When "Expensive" Software Is Worth It

Pay more for tax software when:
- **You have complex income** (W-2 + investments + self-employment)
- **You have state-specific deductions** the cheap option might miss
- **The refund difference exceeds the price difference** (this is the only test that matters)
- **You've already completed the return** and switching means re-entering everything
- **You need audit support** and the paid option includes it

## When "Free" Software Wins

Use free/cheap software when:
- **Simple return** (W-2 income only, standard deduction, no investments)
- **You've compared results** and the refund is the same or higher
- **You're comfortable entering manual adjustments** for items the software misses
- **You understand your state's specific rules** and can verify them yourself

## Red Flags That Your Software Is Missing Something

- Your refund is significantly lower than last year (and your income/situation hasn't changed much)
- You have overtime income but don't see an OT deduction line
- You have kids but the Child Tax Credit shows $0
- Your state refund seems too low (check for state-specific deductions)
- You have a dependent parent but no Other Dependent Credit

## The Process

1. File with the software you used last year (it has your info already — easier, faster)
2. If your situation changed significantly, run a second software as a check
3. Compare bottom lines, not prices
4. If the difference is more than the cost difference, switch
5. If the difference is less than $100, stick with what you know (your time has value)

## Remember

- Tax software is a tool. The tool that gets you the biggest return is the best tool.
- "Free" that misses $2,000 costs you $2,000.
- Running two programs takes 2-3 hours. That's potentially $1,000+/hour of your time.
- Don't be loyal to a brand. Be loyal to your bank account.
